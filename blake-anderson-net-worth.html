Blake Anderson Full Overview and Wiki

Early Beginnings

Born in Sacramento County, California, on March 2, 1984, his journey to stardom began at a young age. After graduating from Clayton Valley High School in Concord, he moved to Los Angeles to pursue his passion for comedy.

Here, he honed his skills with groups like the Upright Citizens Brigade and The Groundlings. His academic journey took him to Orange Coast College in Costa Mesa, where he met future collaborator Adam DeVine.

Rise to Fame

In 2006, Anderson’s professional career took off when he co-founded the sketch comedy troupe, Mail Order Comedy.

The group gained YouTube notoriety, paving the way for their Comedy Central hit Workaholics, which Anderson co-created and starred in from 2011 to 2017.

This series became a cult favorite, spotlighting Anderson’s unique comedic style and solidifying his place in the entertainment industry.

Expanding Horizons

Beyond Workaholics, Anderson didn’t slow down. He appeared in various television shows including Community, Parks and Recreation, and Arrested Development.

His versatility was further showcased in films such as Dope and Game Over, Man! Anderson’s comedic talent, combined with his knack for screenwriting and production, helped him to expand his career beyond acting into voice acting and producing.

Entrepreneurial Ventures

Apart from his entertainment career, Anderson ventured into the fashion industry. In 2013, he co-created the fashion line Teenage with his then-wife, Rachael Finley.

This move not only showcased his entrepreneurial spirit but also diversified his income streams, contributing to his net worth.

Personal Life and Influence

Anderson’s personal life, marked by his marriage and subsequent divorce from Rachael Finley, along with his role as a father to two daughters, has kept him in the public eye.
